Time for an Action!

Do you know how much water was used to grow your food and to produce your clothes and the things you buy?

The fashion industry is estimated to be responsible for about 20% of global wastewater and 10% of global carbon emissions. Water is used in various stages of clothing production, including growing raw materials like cotton, dyeing and finishing fabrics, and washing and treating finished garments. Cotton production alone accounts for the majority of the water footprint in the textile industry.

For example, it takes about 2,700 liters of water to produce enough cotton for one t-shirt. The dyeing and finishing processes also require significant amounts of water. Efforts are being made to reduce water usage and improve water management practices in the industry through initiatives like water recycling, using more sustainable materials, and implementing more efficient production processes.

Additional Points on Cotton Production:

  • Choose clothing made from organic cotton to reduce water pollution and soil contamination.
  • Support sustainable farming practices that prioritize water conservation and minimize environmental impact.
  • Consider the water footprint of different cotton products when making purchasing decisions.
  • Encourage the adoption of rainwater harvesting and drip irrigation systems in cotton farming.
  • Advocate for policies that promote sustainable cotton farming practices and ensure fair wages for farmers.

Additional Statistics on Water Usage:

Many fashion brands are working to improve their water stewardship through initiatives like water recycling and use of less water-intensive materials. Consumers can also take steps to reduce the water footprint associated with the fashion industry:

  • Buy fewer clothes and opt for quality over quantity.
  • Choose more sustainable materials like organic cotton or recycled polyester.
  • Wash clothes less frequently and in full loads.
  • Support brands committed to water stewardship.
  • Participate in clothing rental or resale programs.
